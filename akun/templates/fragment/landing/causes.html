Causes of Stunting

Nutritional Causes

Inadequate nutrition during pregnancy and the first two years of life is the primary cause of stunting. This includes:

  • Insufficient protein and micronutrients in diet
  • Poor breastfeeding practices
  • Inappropriate complementary feeding
  • Frequent infections that reduce nutrient absorption
  • Maternal malnutrition affecting fetal development

Environmental Causes

Poor living conditions contribute significantly to stunting through:

  • Lack of access to clean water and sanitation
  • Poor hygiene practices leading to frequent infections
  • Exposure to environmental toxins
  • Food insecurity due to climate change or economic factors
  • Overcrowded living conditions

Social Causes

Social determinants play a crucial role in child development:

  • Poverty limiting access to nutritious food and healthcare
  • Low maternal education affecting care practices
  • Gender inequality in nutrition allocation
  • Lack of access to healthcare services
  • Inadequate childcare and stimulation
